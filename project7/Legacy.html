Syracuse University’s men’s basketball team was founded in 1900 and quickly established itself as a cornerstone of college basketball. The team’s early years were marked by steady growth, but it was during the mid-20th century that Syracuse began to solidify its reputation as a powerhouse. Key milestones include winning its first NCAA tournament game in 1957 and its first national championship in 2003. Over time, Syracuse basketball has grown into one of the most recognized and respected programs in NCAA Division I basketball, known for its competitive spirit and passionate fan base.

Early Years (1900 – 1950): The team was founded in 1898 and played its first official season in 1899. During these early years, the team laid the foundation for future success, with notable achievements including several conference championships.

Coaching Legacy

One of the most significant contributors to Syracuse’s success is Jim Boeheim, who served as head coach for over 40 years. Boeheim’s leadership transformed Syracuse into a national powerhouse, with over 1,000 career wins, five Final Four appearances, and the program’s first national championship in 2003. His use of the 2-3 zone defense became a signature strategy that revolutionized the game. Other notable coaches include Roy Danforth, who helped elevate the program in the 1970s, and Bernie Fine, a long-time assistant coach who contributed to Syracuse's development.
